Research Interests

  • Community-based management of environmental challenges
  • Urban wildlife ecology and conservation
  • Translation of research into education and public outreach, and public practice

Personal Statement

While pursuing my career, I have had the opportunity to work in diverse ecosystems, from the jungles of the upper Amazonian rainforest, the paramo of the Andes, the tropical dry forest of the Pacific coastal lowlands and the volcanic Galapagos Islands, to the Sonoran Desert. I was afforded these experiences because I come from one of the most megadiverse countries in the world, Ecuador.

Currently, I work for the Central Arizona Conservation Alliance – Desert Botanical Garden developing invasive species management plans for urban protected areas in Phoenix. In addition, I am creating a rare plant citizen science survey and monitoring protocols in a collaborative project between the Desert Botanical Garden and the Tonto National Forest.
